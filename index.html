Our Enhanced Risk Mitigation Strategy

1 . Multiple Trends

Three trends of varying periodicity (to minimize correlation) are monitored and deployed at the same time, with each tranche following its own trend. Long/short sequences are placed within each tranche according to its current trend.

2. Managing Drawdowns

If a currency pair within a given tranche incurs a drawdown, the probability of uncorrelated tranches incurring the same drawdown is decreased due to varying trend periodicity. Example: A long trend on an hourly chart could, at the same time, be a short trend on a daily chart.

3 . Tranche Retracement

Because each tranche has a different expectation of retracement depending on when its trend started, and of which period is being monitored, countertrend sequences will close at different times and levels. The model continues to manage countertrend positions within each tranche. New trade sequences will be opened only in the direction of the tranche‚Äôs current trend. Each tranche can have only one long and one short sequence open at a given time.

4 . Loss Minimization

Given that we have three (3) minimally correlated tranches, it is statistically unlikely that all three (3) will experience a draw down at the same time. When a given tranche reaches its maximum draw down, a stop loss is taken within that tranche only, thereby representing only one-third (1/3) of our total exposure.
